About This Tool

This retirement planning tool uses Monte Carlo simulation to model the uncertainty of investment returns and inflation. For each simulation, it randomly selects historical S&P 500 returns and inflation rates from the past 100 years and applies them to each year of your retirement period. By running thousands of these randomized scenarios, it helps you understand the probability of your retirement plan succeeding under different market conditions.

Retirement Planning Tool Help

How does this tool work?

This tool uses Monte Carlo simulation to model thousands of possible retirement scenarios based on historical market data. Each simulation randomly samples from historical S&P 500 returns and inflation rates to create a unique sequence of returns for your retirement period.

The tool then calculates how your portfolio would perform under each scenario, accounting for:

  • Your initial portfolio balance
  • Annual withdrawals adjusted for inflation
  • Additional income sources during retirement
  • Taxes on withdrawals
  • Market volatility and sequence of returns risk

The success rate represents the percentage of simulations where your money lasted throughout your entire retirement period.

What assumptions does the tool make?

  • Investment Mix: The tool assumes your portfolio is invested in a mix similar to the S&P 500 index.
  • Historical Data: Future returns and inflation are modeled based on historical patterns from 1928 to present.
  • Withdrawal Strategy: The tool assumes you withdraw the same inflation-adjusted amount each year.
  • Taxes: Taxes are simplified as a flat percentage of withdrawals.
  • Rebalancing: The tool assumes your portfolio is regularly rebalanced to maintain your target asset allocation.

How should I interpret the results?

The success rate is the key metric to focus on:

  • 90%+ success rate: Your plan is very robust and can likely withstand significant market downturns.
  • 75-90% success rate: Your plan is reasonably secure but could benefit from some adjustments.
  • 50-75% success rate: Your plan has significant risk and should be revised.
  • Below 50% success rate: Your plan needs major revisions to be viable.

Remember that even a high success rate doesn't guarantee success, and a low rate doesn't guarantee failure. The tool helps you understand the range of possible outcomes based on historical patterns.

How can I improve my success rate?

Several strategies can improve your retirement plan's success rate:

  • Reduce annual expenses to lower your withdrawal rate
  • Work longer to increase savings and reduce the retirement period
  • Add income sources like part-time work or rental income
  • Consider a dynamic withdrawal strategy that adjusts based on market performance
  • Optimize your investment mix for your specific situation
  • Plan for lower expenses in later retirement as spending often decreases with age
